My Ideal Job


Pega BPM Lead Systems Architect

I came across an advertisement for the job position of “Pega BPM Lead Systems Architect”, and was very intrigued by what the position entailed. This seems to be a very demanding role, requiring a lot of experience in coding, IT systems architecture and ‘Dev Ops’, project management, security and business. It seems that a lead IT systems architect is a job that requires a vast knowledge of the intersections between many business and IT related fields, making this job equally daunting and enticing. As I love exploring the ‘inter-relationships of things’, this position speaks highly to my interests. The skills required to perform well in this role include:

  • Knowledge and experience with Scrum and Agile project management methodology
  • Familiarity with Java, J2EE architecture, Extensible Markup Language (XML), JavaServer Pages (JSP), Enterprise JavaBeans (EJB)
  • Familiarity with web languages including Javascript, CSS, HTML5, XML
  • A clear understanding of business, corporate structure, the general and unique needs of the business, and an understanding of accounting to manage budget and scope
  • A good knowledge of IT security, databases, legacy systems and web services
  • A good knowledge of data structures
On top of this fairly overwhelming list, some qualifications and experience are required:
  • A degree in software engineering, computer science or similar
  • A Pega Certification
  • Experience in systems architecture and project management
  • Work experience as a PEGA Developer
All of this would be with the accompanying requirement of a few decades of work experience in information technology, or as a network administrator, systems or software engineer.

Currently, my experiences and knowledge pale in comparison to this list. I have a basic understanding of HTML and CSS, and an introductory knowledge to Python and programming principles. I have experience with small level project management, and believe I have strong communication and leadership skills. As it stands, there is a lot to be desired about my technical capabilities in IT, but I believe the pathway ahead to such a job position is quite clear. I’m currently enrolled in a Bachelor of IT through RMIT to develop a foundation of coding, and a broader understanding of IT fields and practices. After which I could start working in IT to gain experience. Right now I’m not entirely sure what work exactly that might be, but I imagine something in coding, maybe app development, systems administrating or cybersecurity. While working, I could study an MBA to better round out my knowledge of business and accounting, or a masters in Information Systems. If required, I could complement my learning with online project management courses (such as this online project management course.)
